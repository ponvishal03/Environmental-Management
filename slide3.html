Geographic Context

A coastal megacity situated on Salsette Island, bounded by the Arabian Sea to the west and Thane Creek to the east. The city's linear geography creates unique environmental management challenges.

603 km² Total Area
149 km Coastline

Demographics & Economy

~21 M Metro Population
~30,000 People / km² (Density)

India's financial capital and a major port hub. High population density exerts immense pressure on infrastructure, waste management, and open spaces.

Key Natural Assets

  • Mithi River: 18 km river flowing from Vihar/Powai lakes to Mahim Creek.
  • Mangroves: ~59 km² cover acting as critical flood buffers and carbon sinks.
  • Coastal Zone: Extensive network of creeks (Mahim, Malad, Gorai) and beaches.
  • Sanjay Gandhi National Park: A unique protected forest within city limits.
